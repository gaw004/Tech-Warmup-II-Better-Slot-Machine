# User Research

## Personas

### Persona 1 - Marcus (Casual Casino Player)

- **Name**: Marcus
- **Age**: 35
- **Relationship**: single
- **Occupation**: Accountant
- **Location**: San Diego, CA
- **Background**:

  Marcus visits the Barona Casino a few times a year with his friends and enjoys playing slots as a way to relieve stress from his work. He's not addictive, but he loves playing slot machine at the casino. Lately, his work has been extremely stressful and busy, and he barely has any time to do anything after work. He can't make it out to the casino, so he started playing slot machine game on his phone as a quick way to relax and scratch the itch of missing the real casino experiment. Also, he love out of space theme slot machines, and he spends a lot of time playing it.

- **Goal**:
  - He wants to have fun and feels like he's at the casino from his bed.
  - He wants the play quickly without reading many instructions of how to play the game.
  - He want to feel like his choices in the game actually make a difference of outcomes.
- **He's frustrated when**
  - Games look cheap because casino slot machines are fun and have strong visual theme.
  - Screen has too many buttons that he doesn't know what each button does.
  - Slots have no special features (no bonus games, no special effects or music).

### Persona 2 - Noah (Online Gambler)

- **Name**: Noah
- **Age**: 23
- **Relationship**: In a relationship
- **Occupation**: Student
- **Location**: Austin, Texas
- **Background**:

  Noah grew up in a wealthy suburb of Southern California and currently attends flight school part time. With his family covering his major expenses, he has more disposable income and free time than most people his age — and he's always looking for ways to fill both.
Gambling is Noah's go-to hobby. He enjoys poker, blackjack, baccarat, and slots, and gravitates toward online platforms because they're always available whenever boredom strikes. For Noah, gambling isn't about the money — it's about the rush. He wants to feel the high of a big win and stay visually engaged throughout.

**What he wants from an online gambling experience:**

- Games that feel exciting even on smaller bets — big animations, sound effects, and celebratory moments
- Mini-games and bonus rounds that break up the monotony and keep sessions feeling fresh
- Fast-paced gameplay that holds his attention
- Easy replayability - big and responsive respin button

**What frustrates him:**

- Slow or clunky interfaces that kill the momentum
- Games that feel repetitive after a few rounds
- Complicated deposit or withdrawal processes
- Boring game backgrounds and icons

**How he plays:**

- Primarily on his pc, usually in the evenings or during downtime between flight lessons
- Sessions range from 20 minutes to a couple of hours depending on how engaged he is
- Browses between multiple games rather than committing to one
- Likes to stream his sessions on discord so he can enjoy playing with his friends

### Persona 3 - Bardow Lewis (Slot Machine Player)
* **Name**: Bardow Lewis
* **Age**: Likely 20s-30s
* **Relationship**: Unknown
* **Occupation**: Dealer Student / Former Tribal Casino Dealer
* **Location**: Las Vegas (Relocated from Seattle)
* **Background**: Bardow is a Native American who previously worked as a dealer at a tribal casino in Seattle and recently relocated to Las Vegas to attend dealer school. His ultimate ambition is to work at the luxury casinos on the Las Vegas Strip. Interestingly, despite knowing the odds and refusing to play table games because he has seen too many people lose, he is heavily addicted to slot machines because he also has seen many people won the jackpot. He often falls into the trap of the "casino money effect," where he recklessly gambles away his winnings because he doesn't view it as his hard-earned money.
* **Goal**:
    * To graduate from dealer school and become a table games dealer at a luxury casino on the Strip.
    * To experience the thrill of the game and hopefully hit a massive slot machine jackpot one day.
    * To win back the money he previously lost to the machines.
* **He's frustrated when**:
    * He loses an entire two-week paycheck (e.g., $1,000) to the slot machines in a single session.
    * He desperately tries to just "break even" and ends up draining his savings account in the process.
    * He realizes that as a dealer, he has absolutely no control over the mathematical outcome of the games.

### Persona 4 - Scott Tomlinson (Advantage/Recreational Player)
* **Name**: Scott Tomlinson
* **Age**: Middle-aged
* **Relationship**: Unknown
* **Occupation**: Casino Poker Room Supervisor
* **Location**: Las Vegas
* **Background**: Scott is a highly rational casino poker room supervisor at a luxury Las Vegas casino who has been playing casino games almost every day for the past 22 to 23 years. Unlike problem gamblers, he plays strictly for fun with low stakes (usually bringing only $60 to $100) and treats it as a game of strategy rather than a way to make money. In his free time, he spends hours watching gambling videos on YouTube while cooking or walking his dog, using them to study the math, payout frequencies, and strategies of various slot machines.
* **Goal**:
    * To enjoy the entertainment, fun, and strategic aspects of gambling without risking his financial stability.
    * To continuously figure out the math and payout regularity behind different slot machines by analyzing YouTube videos.
    * To make his small budget stretch for hours of gameplay without losing it all.
* **He's frustrated when**:
    * Uneducated tourists show up at the casino with a false sense of entitlement and lose their money because they have no plan.
    * Players fall victim to cognitive distortions, believing their past losses mean a machine is "due" to pay them back.
    * Gamblers fail to educate themselves on the mathematical reality of the house edge.

### Persona 5 - Retired Old Lady (Senior Promotional Player)
* **Name**: Molly White
* **Age**: In her 80s
* **Relationship**: Married
* **Occupation**: Retired
* **Location**: Las Vagas suburban
* **Background**: This retired woman in her 80s has been visiting Las Vegas with her husband every year for the past 26 years. After living a highly frugal life and working hard for decades to build her savings, she feels she no longer needs to save for the future and uses the casino as her primary form of twilight entertainment. She meticulously tracks casino promotions, such as "50 Plus Club" days, BOGO meals, and free play, allowing the casino to serve as a sort of luxurious, albeit calculated, companion in her old age.
* **Goal**:
    * To find an engaging, socially active way to spend her retirement years with her husband.
    * To strictly adhere to her daily gambling budget of around $100.
    * To maximize the value of senior citizen casino promotions, comps, and free perks.
* **She's frustrated when**:
    * She and her husband hit a losing streak and feel they have put too much money into a single machine.
    * The emotional sting of losing tempts them to go over their strict $100 daily limit.
    * The games fail to provide the extended entertainment value she expects for her budget.
 
  
## User Stories

## User Story 1 - Casual Player

As a casual player, I want to understand how to play the game immediately without reading much instructions, so that I can jump into the game and play right away. The interface should be obvious where to spin, how much I'm betting, and how much money I have left. If I win, I want a clear but satisfying visual reaction so I can feel excited that I just won.

## User Story 2 - Stealth Interface & Privacy Features

As a frequent gambler who struggles to maintain transparency with my family regarding my habits, I want a discreet, mobile-accessible slot machine interface, so that I can engage in gaming privately and avoid the immediate detection and domestic conflict that arises from my gambling.

## User Story 3 - Paylines

As a player, I want to see lines connecting matching symbols, so I can understand at a glance how successful my spin was.
